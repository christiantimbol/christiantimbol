About

In some words:

Born and raised in San Jose, California, I am an Industrial & Systems Engineer (ISE) who found a passion for Software Development that graduated with a Bachelor's Degree in ISE from San Jose State University (SJSU) on May 2017. I've done numerous projects (academic, extracurricular, corporate, and personal) and taken many courses revolving around Industrial & Systems Engineering, as well as Software Development.

At SJSU, I was a member of many organizations, such as the Boxing Club, Robotics Club (Mechanical Team, Mission Control Design Team, and Lead of Business), Delta Upsilon Fraternity (Chair for Philanthropy), Institute of Industrial & Systems Engineers (IISE), Orientation and Transition Leaders (OAT), and Educational Opportunity Program (EOP).

The awards I've received while at SJSU are: the A.S. 55 Award in March of 2015 (for demonstrating an outstanding commitment to the SJSU community), the Four Founding Principles Scholarship in April of 2015 (for most successfully demonstrating the Founding Principles of Delta Upsilon), 2nd Place in the Intel Rapid Prototyping Challenge in December of 2015 (for developing a prototype navigational device for the visually-impaired), and Dean's Scholar in April of 2017 (for earning 3.65+ GPA for two contiguous semesters).

I have interned at an Aerospace Company called Space Systems Loral in Palo Alto, California for a year (May 2016 - May 2017). Working there allowed me to use Industrial & Systems Engineering principles that I learned in school to develop software tools that supported process improvement initatives.
